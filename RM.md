# 12. 🔍 INDUCTION vs DEDUCTION

EXTREMELY IMPORTANT.

---

# 🔹 Induction

### 💡 Meaning

Specific observations → general conclusion.

---

# 🔥 Example

Seeing many white swans →
concluding swans are white.

---

# 🔹 Deduction

### 💡 Meaning

General principle → specific conclusion.

---

# 🔥 Example

All humans are mortal
Socrates is human
→ Socrates is mortal

---

# 🔥 VERY IMPORTANT COMPARISON

| Induction          | Deduction          |
| ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Specific → General | General → Specific |
| Observation-based  | Theory-based       |

---

# 🔥 Viva

👉 “Difference between induction and deduction?”

Induction moves from observations to theory, while deduction applies general theory to specific cases.

# 5. 📉 MEASURES OF VARIABILITY

VERY IMPORTANT.

---

# 💡 Definition

Measure spread/distribution of data.

---

# 🔹 Range

Difference between:

```text id="zbpn9q"
maximum - minimum
```

---

# 🔹 Variance

Measures average squared deviation.

Formula:
\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (x-\mu)^2}{N}

---

# 🔹 Standard Deviation

Square root of variance.

Formula:
\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2}

---

# 🔥 Meaning

Higher standard deviation →
more spread.

---

# 🔥 Viva

👉 “What does standard deviation measure?”

Spread/dispersion of data.

# 9. 🧪 HYPOTHESIS TESTING (MOST IMPORTANT)

EXTREMELY IMPORTANT.

---

# 💡 Definition

Statistical method used to verify assumptions about data.

---

# 🔥 Components

---

## Null Hypothesis (H₀)

Assumes:

```text id="z43f6v"
No effect / no difference
```

---

## Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

Assumes:

```text id="5x2q2n"
Effect / difference exists
```

---

# 🔥 Example

H₀:

```text id="5johx2"

```

H₁:

```text id="ccsgfx"

```

---

# 🔥 Steps in Hypothesis Testing

1. Define hypotheses
2. Collect data
3. Perform statistical test
4. Accept/reject H₀

---

# 🔥 Viva

👉 “What is null hypothesis?”

Default assumption that no relationship/effect exists.

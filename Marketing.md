## **UNIT 1: INTRODUCTION AND MARKETING ENVIRONMENT (12 Hours)**

---

## 1. **Marketing ‚Äì Nature, Scope and Importance**

### **Meaning of Marketing**

Marketing is the process of **identifying customer needs and wants**, **creating value**, **communicating that value**, and **delivering satisfaction profitably**.

> According to Philip Kotler:
> *‚ÄúMarketing is a social and managerial process by which individuals and groups obtain what they need and want through creating and exchanging value.‚Äù*

---

### **Nature of Marketing**

Marketing has the following nature:

1. **Customer-Oriented** ‚Äì Focuses on customer satisfaction.
2. **Continuous Process** ‚Äì Starts before production and continues after sales.
3. **Value-Based** ‚Äì Emphasizes value creation rather than mere selling.
4. **Integrated Activity** ‚Äì Includes product, price, promotion, and distribution.
5. **Dynamic** ‚Äì Changes with market conditions and customer preferences.

---

### **Scope of Marketing**

The scope of marketing includes:

* Goods (FMCG, durables)
* Services (banking, education, healthcare)
* Ideas (Swachh Bharat)
* Events (sports events, concerts)
* Places (tourism marketing)
* Organizations (brand image building)

---

### **Importance of Marketing**

Marketing is important because it:

* Identifies customer needs
* Facilitates exchange
* Increases sales and profits
* Creates employment
* Helps economic development
* Builds brand loyalty

---

## 2. **Evolution of Marketing**

Marketing has evolved through different stages:

### 1. **Production Era**

* Focus: Mass production
* Assumption: Consumers prefer easily available products
* Example: Early industrial period

### 2. **Product Era**

* Focus: Quality and features
* Risk: Marketing myopia

### 3. **Selling Era**

* Focus: Aggressive selling and promotion
* Goal: Push products into the market

### 4. **Marketing Era**

* Focus: Customer needs and satisfaction
* Strategy: Produce what customers want

### 5. **Societal Marketing Era**

* Focus: Customer + society welfare
* Example: Eco-friendly products

---

## 3. **Core Marketing Concepts**

### 1. **Needs, Wants and Demands**

* Needs: Basic human requirements
* Wants: Needs shaped by culture
* Demands: Wants backed by purchasing power

### 2. **Market**

A market consists of buyers and sellers exchanging goods/services.

### 3. **Exchange**

Exchange is the act of obtaining a desired product by offering something in return.

### 4. **Value and Satisfaction**

Value = Benefits ‚Äì Cost
Customer satisfaction depends on perceived value.

---

## 4. **Company Orientation Concepts**

### 1. **Production Concept**

* Focus: High production efficiency
* Assumption: Consumers want cheap and available products

### 2. **Product Concept**

* Focus: Quality and innovation
* Risk: Ignoring customer needs

### 3. **Selling Concept**

### 4. **Marketing Concept**

### 5. **Holistic Marketing Concept**

Includes:

* Internal marketing

* Integrated marketing
* Social responsibility marketing

## **1. Market Segmentation**

### **Meaning of Market Segmentation**

Market segmentation is the process of **dividing a large, heterogeneous market into smaller, homogeneous groups of consumers** who have **similar needs, preferences, and buying behaviour**, so that each segment can be served more effectively.

> **Definition:**
> ‚ÄúMarket segmentation is the subdividing of a market into homogeneous subsets of customers, where each subset may be selected as a target market.‚Äù ‚Äì Philip Kotler

---

### **Need / Importance of Market Segmentation**

Market segmentation is important because:

1. Consumers differ in needs, income, tastes, and preferences
2. Firms cannot satisfy all consumers with a single product
3. Helps in better product design
4. Efficient use of marketing resources
5. Improves customer satisfaction
6. Helps firms gain competitive advantage

---

### **Levels of Market Segmentation**

1. **Mass Marketing**

   * One product for entire market
   * Example: Salt, sugar

2. **Segment Marketing**

   * Different products for different segments
   * Example: Different shampoos for men/women

3. **Niche Marketing**

   * Focus on a small, specialized segment
   * Example: Luxury watches

4. **Local Marketing**

   * Products customized for local markets
   * Example: McDonald‚Äôs menu in India

5. **Individual Marketing**

   * Customized products for individual customers
   * Example: Personalized ads online

---

### **Bases for Segmenting Consumer Markets**

---

### **1. Geographic Segmentation**

Market is divided based on **geographical factors** such as:

* Region
* Country
* Climate
* Urban/Rural areas

**Example:**
Woollen clothes sold more in North India than South India.

---

### **2. Demographic Segmentation**

Segmentation based on:

* Age
* Gender
* Income
* Occupation
* Education
* Family size

**Example:**
Children‚Äôs toys targeted at age group 3‚Äì10 years.

---

### **3. Psychographic Segmentation**

Based on:

* Lifestyle
* Personality
* Social class
* Values

**Example:**
Fitness brands targeting health-conscious consumers.

---

### **4. Behavioural Segmentation**

Based on consumer behaviour such as:

* Usage rate
* Brand loyalty
* Benefits sought
* Occasion

**Example:**
Discount offers during festivals.

---

## **2. Market Targeting**

---

### **Meaning of Targeting**

Market targeting refers to the process of **evaluating different market segments and selecting one or more segments to enter and serve**.

---

### **Factors Considered While Selecting Target Market**

1. Size and growth of segment

3. Competition

### **Targeting Strategies**

### **2. Differentiated Marketing**

* Different products for different segments

**Example:**
Different variants of toothpaste.

---

### **3. Concentrated Marketing**

* Focus on one specific segment
* Suitable for small firms

**Example:**
Luxury sports cars.

---

### **4. Micromarketing**

* Tailored marketing for individuals or local markets

**Example:**
Online personalized recommendations.

---

Positioning is the process of **creating a distinct and desirable image of a product in the minds of target customers**, relative to competing products.

> ‚ÄúPositioning is not what you do to the product, but what you do to the mind of the consumer.‚Äù

1. Identify target market

### **Bases of Positioning**

* Rapid sales growth
* Increasing profits

**Strategy:**
Improve product quality, expand distribution

### **3. Maturity Stage**

* Sales peak
* Intense competition
* Price reduction

**Strategy:**
Product modification, market modification

---

### **4. Decline Stage**

* Falling sales
* Reduced profits

**Strategy:**
Discontinue or rejuvenate product

## **1. Meaning of Price and Pricing**

### **Meaning of Price**

Price is the **amount of money charged for a product or service**, or the value that customers give up in exchange for the benefits of using a product.

> **Definition:**
> ‚ÄúPrice is the sum of values that consumers exchange for the benefits of having or using a product or service.‚Äù

---

### **Meaning of Pricing**

Pricing is the process of **fixing the price of a product or service** after considering cost, demand, competition, and other factors.

---

### **Importance of Pricing**

Pricing is important because:

1. Price determines **sales volume and profits**
2. Only element of marketing mix that **generates revenue**
3. Influences consumer perception
4. Affects company‚Äôs competitive position
5. Helps in market penetration or skimming

---

## **2. Determinants of Price**

Pricing decisions are influenced by **internal and external factors**.

---

### **A. Internal Factors**

1. **Cost of Production**

   * Fixed and variable costs
2. **Marketing Objectives**

   * Profit maximization
   * Market penetration
3. **Product Characteristics**

   * Quality, uniqueness
4. **Marketing Mix Strategy**

   * Price consistency with product, promotion, place

---

### **B. External Factors**

1. **Demand**

   * Price elasticity of demand
2. **Competition**

   * Competitor pricing
3. **Government Regulations**

   * Price controls, taxes
4. **Economic Conditions**

   * Inflation, income levels

---

### **2. Value-Based Pricing**

Price based on **customer‚Äôs perceived value**

**Example:**
Premium brands charging higher prices.

---

### **3. Competition-Based Pricing**

Price fixed according to competitor prices.

### **4. Psychological Pricing**

# **UNIT 4: MARKETING OF SERVICES**

*(9 Hours | Short unit but very scoring)*

---

## **1. Meaning of Services**

### **Definition of Service**

A service is **an intangible activity or benefit** that one party offers to another, which **does not result in ownership of anything**, and is usually **produced and consumed simultaneously**.

> **Definition (Kotler):**
> ‚ÄúA service is any activity or benefit that one party can offer to another that is essentially intangible and does not result in the ownership of anything.‚Äù

---

### **Examples of Services**

* Banking
* Education
* Healthcare
* Transportation
* Tourism
* Insurance

---

## **2. Difference Between Goods and Services**

| Basis                    | Goods         | Services         |
| ------------------------ | ------------- | ---------------- |
| Tangibility              | Tangible      | Intangible       |
| Ownership                | Transferable  | No ownership     |
| Storage                  | Can be stored | Cannot be stored |
| Production & Consumption | Separate      | Simultaneous     |
| Standardization          | Possible      | Difficult        |

*(This table is very useful in exams)*

---

## **3. Characteristics of Services**

This is the **most important question** of Unit 4.

---

### **1. Intangibility**

Services cannot be **seen, touched, or felt** before purchase.

**Example:**
Medical consultation, teaching.

**Marketing implication:**
Use branding, physical evidence, testimonials.

---

### **2. Inseparability**

Production and consumption of services happen **at the same time**.

**Example:**
Teacher and student interaction in class.

---

### **3. Variability (Heterogeneity)**

Service quality **varies from person to person** and situation to situation.

**Example:**
Different experience with the same restaurant staff.

---

### **4. Perishability**

Services **cannot be stored** for future sale.

**Example:**
Empty airline seats cannot be sold later.

---

### **5. Lack of Ownership**

Consumers do not own services after purchase.

**Example:**
Hotel stay, movie ticket.

---

## **4. Marketing Strategies for Service Firms ‚Äì 7Ps**

Traditional marketing mix (4Ps) is **not sufficient** for services.
Hence, **3 more Ps** are added.

---

## **A. Traditional 4Ps**

---

### **1. Product (Service Product)**

Includes:

* Core service
* Supplementary services

**Example:**
Banking ‚Äì account + ATM + online banking.

---

### **2. Price**

Pricing depends on:

* Demand
* Competition
* Perceived value

**Example:**
Higher fees for premium hospital rooms.

---

### **3. Place**

Refers to **service delivery channels**.

**Example:**
Online education platforms.

---

### **4. Promotion**

Includes:

* Advertising
* Word of mouth
* Digital marketing

---

## **B. Additional 3Ps (Very Important)**

---

### **5. People**

People are the **service providers** who interact with customers.

**Importance:**

* Skills
* Attitude
* Behaviour

**Example:**
Hotel staff behaviour affecting customer satisfaction.

---

### **6. Process**

Process refers to **how service is delivered**.

**Example:**
Steps involved in airline ticket booking.

---

### **7. Physical Evidence**

Physical evidence includes **tangible cues** that help customers evaluate services.

**Example:**
Hospital building, staff uniforms, ambience.

---

## **5. Importance of Service Marketing**

Service marketing is important because:

1. Service sector contributes major share to GDP
2. Growing demand for services
3. Customer satisfaction depends heavily on service quality
4. Services are people-intensive

---
